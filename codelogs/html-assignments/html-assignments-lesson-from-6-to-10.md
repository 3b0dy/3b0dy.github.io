### Assignment 6

```html
<!DOCTYPE html>
<html lang="en">
  <head>
    <meta charset="UTF-8" />
    <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0" />
    <title>3b0dy's Bookstore</title>
  </head>
  <body>
    <main class="bookstore">
      <h1 class="store-title">3b0dy's Bookstore</h1>
      <p class="store-desc">
        Welcome to my humble bookstore. <br />
        Here you'll find a wonderful collection of books that will keep you entertained for hours.
      </p>
      <hr />
      <section class="technical-books">
        <h2>Technical</h2>
        <p>These books help you understand the "philosophy" of writing proper code beyond just programming languages.</p>
        <ol>
          <li>
            <h3>Clean Code</h3>
            <p>
              This book is considered the "bible" of programmers; <br />
              it doesn't teach you how to program, but rather how to write clean, <br />
              understandable code that is easy for other programmers to maintain. <br />
              It focuses on variable naming, function organization, and avoiding common mistakes.
            </p>
            <hr />
          </li>
          <li>
            <h3>The Pragmatic Programmer</h3>
            <p>
              This comprehensive book covers the practical aspects of a programmer's life. <br />
              It discusses how to maintain your career path, problem-solving techniques, <br />
              and how to write flexible code that adapts to changes, <br />
              along with invaluable tips for developing your personal skills as a technician.
            </p>
            <hr />
          </li>
          <li>
            <h3>Grokking Algorithms</h3>
            <p>
              If you find algorithms difficult to understand, this book is the answer. <br />
              It uses illustrations and images to simplify the most complex mathematical and programming concepts, <br />
              making it very enjoyable to understand the "logic" behind data organization and searching.
            </p>
          </li>
        </ol>
      </section>
      <hr />
      <section class="personal-development-books">
        <h2>Personal Development</h2>
        <p>Learning requires "patience," and these books will teach you how to build a daily study routine without getting bored.</p>
        <ol>
          <li>
            <h3>Atomic Habits</h3>
            <p>
              This book explains how very simple (atomic) changes you make daily, <br />
              such as studying for just 10 minutes,can lead to amazing long-term results. <br />
              It's the perfect book for anyone struggling to stick to a study schedule.
            </p>
            <hr />
          </li>
          <li>
            <h3>Deep Work</h3>
            <p>
              In an age of distractions (Facebook, mobile notifications), <br />
              this book teaches you how to focus completely and with maximum mental energy on one complex task (such as learning programming), <br />
              and how to accomplish in two hours what others accomplish in a whole day.
            </p>
            <hr />
          </li>
          <li>
            <h3>A Mind for Numbers</h3>
            <p>
              This book is specifically designed for those studying scientific and technical fields. <br />
              It explains how the human mind works while absorbing difficult information and offers practical strategies for overcoming procrastination and understanding complex lessons more quickly.
            </p>
          </li>
        </ol>
      </section>
    </main>
  </body>
</html>
```
